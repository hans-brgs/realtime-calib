# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

To keep the [dual-licensing model](/docs/contributing/license#commercial-use)
possible, every external contributor must agree to a **Contributor License
Agreement** before their contribution can be merged.

## Why a CLA?

Because realtime-calib is offered both under **AGPL-3.0** and under a **commercial
license**, the project needs the right to relicense contributed code. Without a
CLA, contributed code could only ever be AGPL, which would break the ability to
offer commercial licenses over the whole codebase.

## What you grant

By signing, you confirm that:

- you are the author of your contribution (or have the right to submit it), and
- you grant Hans Bourgeois (Myosin) the rights needed to distribute your
  contribution under **both** the AGPL-3.0 and a commercial license.

You **retain copyright** of your contribution.

:::note Work in progress
The exact CLA text and signing mechanism (e.g. a CLA-assistant bot on pull
requests, or a DCO sign-off) are being finalized. The canonical agreement will
live in [`CLA.md`](https://github.com/hans-brgs/realtime-calib/blob/main/CLA.md) at
the repository root.
:::
